How I came to create the space I once needed myself

I was quiet for a while. Years, actually. Years in which I was busy with work, with life, with everyone else, with the house, the garden, my little animals.

The days went by, unrolling at dizzying speed. I kept promising myself I'd make time for me — but no, I had to stay focused.

And so, once again, I burned out. And I had to recover, to wait for quiet to settle in my mind.

I eventually woke up in that strange place where all roads look equally uncertain and none of them comes with instructions.

I could have kept waiting for the way to reveal itself. I tried that, in fact. It didn't.

So I decided to choose my own road. It sounds brave, written here. In reality it was far less spectacular and far more "what do I want to do now?" said in a low voice early in the morning, waiting for the coffee water to boil.

I think that's where it all started — somewhere in that question.

I came to coaching first because I felt I needed it myself; then I found I wanted to offer sessions of my own, with the certainty that I had found my way. And — for me — it is transformational.

I discovered that good questions do more than advice ever does, that nobody needs to be told what to do — what we need is a space where we can hear what we already know. And I realised that this is what I want to do: to be in that space, alongside someone else.

More precisely, alongside women. Women in exactly that place — who feel something has ended but can't yet see what comes next. Who have done so much and still ask themselves "is this all?". Who want to start something new, and wouldn't mind not being alone while they search for the road.

Because here is the thing I learned the hard way: a crossroads is not crossed by force, but with gentleness — and that gentleness often comes from someone walking beside you.

So I made something. It's called Capitolul următor — "the next chapter" — a coaching package designed precisely for these women, launching on September 1. Six meetings, one per week, in which I don't hand you ready-made solutions; we look for yours, together.

For now I'm opening a few spots, for women who want to begin now. If something resonated while you were reading, let's meet. Half an hour, no obligation — just to see if we're a good fit.
